# 22 — Person: Ezra

**Number:** 22

**Person:** Ezra

**Action:** Reading the law to the people (standing on the platform)

**Object:** Scroll of the law

**Scripture connection:** Nehemiah 8:1–8 — Ezra the scribe brought the Law before the assembly; he read from it from early morning until midday; the people stood and listened.

**Memory hook:** 22 = the twenty-two letters of the Hebrew alphabet. Ezra the scribe — the man of the letters, the one who read the law so that the people could hear and obey. One scroll, one covenant restored.

**Why this number?** 22 letters of the Hebrew alphabet; Ezra the scribe (Nehemiah 8). The number 22 ties the scribe to the letters that make up the Word.
