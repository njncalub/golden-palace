# 15 - ס - Samech (#60)

## The Picture

- **Ancient pictograph:** A prop or support, a pillar that holds up a structure
- **Modern character:** ס (makes an "s" sound)

## The Meaning

The samech is the picture of support, of a foundation that sustains, of divine help, and of the circular nature of God's faithfulness. It represents the upholding power of God, the support that never fails.

- **Prop/Support** — that which upholds, sustains, or maintains
- **Foundation** — the base upon which everything rests
- **Divine support** — God's upholding hand
- **Circle** — the endless cycle, the eternal nature of God's faithfulness

## The Sound

- **Pronunciation:** "S" (like "support" or "salvation")
- **English approximation:** The sibilant s-sound, like support

## The Hook That Will Make This Stick

1. **Samech is a PERFECT CIRCLE.** Look at the letter: ס. It's the only truly circular letter in the Hebrew alphabet. The rabbis say this represents the "cycle of sin" or the "encircling protection" of God. The samech-circle can be a trap of sin or a shield of protection—depending on whether you're inside or outside God's will.

2. **Samech is SIXTY, and sixty is SUPPORT.** The number 60 appears in Scripture as a number of provision and support: Solomon's temple was 60 cubits long (1 Kings 6:2). The city of Jerusalem was built as a city that is compact together—supported, unified (Psalm 122:3). Sixty is the number of sustained blessing.

3. **Samech begins the word SUPPORT (Somek).** The Hebrew root _samak_ means "to lean upon," "to support," "to uphold." Psalm 91:1 says, _"He who dwells in the shelter of the Most High will rest in the shadow of the Almighty."_ The samech-shadow of God's protection covers those who trust in Him.

4. **The SAMECH appears in God's name.** The name _El Shaddai_ (אל שדי) contains a samech-sound. Shaddai means "the Sufficient One" or "the All-Sufficient One." The samech-support of God is enough to hold you up when everything else fails.

## Theological Depth

The support and foundation imagery appears throughout Scripture:

- **The Pillars of the Earth** — 1 Samuel 2:8 — _"For the foundations of the earth are the LORD's; upon them he has set the world."_ The samech-foundation of the world is God Himself.

- **God's Upholding Hand** — Psalm 37:17 — _"For the power of the wicked will be broken, but the LORD upholds the righteous."_ The samech-strength of God sustains His people.

- **The Foundation of God's Throne** — Psalm 93:2 — _"Your throne is established of old; you are from everlasting."_ The samech-foundation of God's rule is eternal.

- **Jesus as the Foundation** — 1 Corinthians 3:11 — _"For no one can lay any foundation other than the one already laid, which is Jesus Christ."_ The samech-support of God is found in Christ alone.

Jesus is our Samech. He is the Foundation that supports all things. He said, _"All things have been committed to me by my Father"_ (Matthew 11:27). The One who upholds the universe by His word (Hebrews 1:3) is the One who holds you up.

## Why You Won't Forget It

Picture a perfect circle drawn in the sand. The circle has no beginning and no end—eternal, complete. Inside the circle, you see a figure—you, safe and protected. Above the circle, the number **60** appears as light. The circle is both a shield and a foundation—supporting and protecting at the same time.

**Circle. Support. Sixty. Eternal.**

That's samech.

## In the Sequence

**[Nun (נ) — Seed](14-nun.md)** gave us life from death; Samech is what holds it up — the prop, the foundation, the circle of God's faithfulness. **Next comes [Ayin (ע) — Eye](16-ayin.md)**: to see, to perceive, the watchful care of God.
