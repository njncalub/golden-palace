# 26 - Ezekiel

**TLDR:** Prophet to the exiles: God's glory leaves the temple; judgment is certain. Then dry bones live, a new temple is shown, and God's glory returns. Visions and signs mark the book.

## Overarching Storyline

Call and visions (ch. 1–3). Signs of siege and exile (ch. 4–7). Glory leaves the temple (ch. 8–11). Oracles and allegories (ch. 12–24). Nations (ch. 25–32). Watchman; restoration (ch. 33–37). Gog and Magog (ch. 38–39). New temple and land (ch. 40–48).

**Bible Project:** [Ezekiel overview](https://bibleproject.com/explore/video/ezekiel/).

## Pegs for Memorizing This Book

- **Person:** Ezekiel, the glory of the LORD.
- **Image:** Wheel within a wheel, dry bones, new temple, river from the temple.
- **Number:** 37 (dry bones in ch. 37), 48 (chapters; temple vision ends book).
- **Phrase:** "Son of man"; "Dry bones" (37); "The glory of the LORD" (43:2).

## Highlights

- Ezekiel 1 — Vision of the throne and wheels.
- Ezekiel 36:26 — New heart and spirit.
- Ezekiel 37 — Valley of dry bones.
- Ezekiel 47 — River from the temple.

## Before and After

- **Before:** Lamentations grieves; Ezekiel prophesies to those already in exile.
- **After:** Daniel is also in exile; Ezekiel's temple vision parallels Revelation's imagery.

## Place in the Overarching Biblical Story

**Glory and restoration.** The Spirit gives new life (ch. 37); the NT applies this to spiritual renewal. The river from the temple (ch. 47) echoes in Revelation 22.

## Interesting Facts

- **Son of man** — God's address to Ezekiel; Jesus uses the title for himself (e.g. Mark 2:10).
- **Dry bones** — Often used for revival and national restoration; the Spirit brings life.
