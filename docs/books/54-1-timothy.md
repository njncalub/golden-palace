# 54 - 1 Timothy

**TLDR:** Paul's charge to Timothy: guard the faith, appoint qualified leaders, and teach sound doctrine. "Fight the good fight of the faith." Instructions for men and women, elders, deacons, and the rich.

## Overarching Storyline

Charge to Timothy; law and gospel (ch. 1). Prayer; men and women (ch. 2). Overseers and deacons (ch. 3). False teachers; godliness (ch. 4). Widows, elders, slaves (ch. 5). Contentment; fight the good fight (ch. 6).

**Bible Project:** [1 Timothy overview](https://bibleproject.com/explore/video/1-timothy/).

## Pegs for Memorizing This Book

- **Person:** Paul, Timothy.
- **Image:** Good fight, deposit of faith, qualifications list.
- **Number:** 6 (chapters), 3 (Pastoral Epistles: 1–2 Tim, Titus).
- **Phrase:** "Fight the good fight" (6:12); "Godliness with contentment" (6:6).

## Highlights

- 1 Timothy 2:5 — One God and one mediator between God and mankind, the man Christ Jesus.
- 1 Timothy 3:1–7 — Qualifications for overseer.
- 1 Timothy 6:10 — Love of money is a root of all kinds of evil.

## Before and After

- **Before:** 2 Thessalonians; 1 Timothy is one of the Pastorals — church order and doctrine.
- **After:** 2 Timothy; 1 Timothy is organizational; 2 Timothy is more personal (Paul's last letter feel).

## Place in the Overarching Biblical Story

**Church order.** How to lead and teach; how to resist false doctrine. The Pastorals shape eldership, deacons, and pastoral responsibility in many traditions.

## Interesting Facts

- **Pastoral Epistles** — 1–2 Timothy and Titus; same themes: sound teaching, qualifications, conduct.
- **1 Timothy 2:12** — Often discussed regarding women and teaching; interpretations vary; context is public worship and authority.
