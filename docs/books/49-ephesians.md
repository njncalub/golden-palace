# 49 - Ephesians

**TLDR:** In Christ we are chosen, redeemed, and sealed; the church is His body and the mystery of Jew and Gentile as one. "For it is by grace you have been saved, through faith — and this is not from yourselves, it is the gift of God."

## Overarching Storyline

Blessing in Christ (ch. 1). Dead in sin, alive in Christ (ch. 2). Mystery: one new humanity (ch. 3). Unity, gifts, maturity (ch. 4). Walk in love, light, wisdom (ch. 5). Armor of God; stand (ch. 6).

**Bible Project:** [Ephesians overview](https://bibleproject.com/explore/video/ephesians/).

## Pegs for Memorizing This Book

- **Person:** Paul, the church (body of Christ).
- **Image:** Heavenly realms, one new humanity, armor of God.
- **Number:** 6 (chapters), 6 (pieces of armor in 6:14–17).
- **Phrase:** "By grace through faith" (2:8); "Armor of God" (6:11).

## Highlights

- Ephesians 2:8–9 — By grace you have been saved through faith; not by works.
- Ephesians 4:32 — Be kind and compassionate; forgive as Christ forgave you.
- Ephesians 6:10–18 — Armor of God: belt, breastplate, shoes, shield, helmet, sword.

## Before and After

- **Before:** Galatians; Ephesians may have been a circular letter (no specific problems); it unfolds the "in Christ" life.
- **After:** Philippians; Ephesians is cosmic and ecclesial; Philippians is personal and joyful.

## Place in the Overarching Biblical Story

**Church and cosmic Christ.** Chosen before creation, united in Christ, walking in the Spirit, and standing in the armor — the letter gives identity and practice for the body of Christ.

## Interesting Facts

- **Circular letter?** — Some early manuscripts omit "in Ephesus" in 1:1; may have been sent to several churches.
- **Armor of God** — Belt of truth, breastplate of righteousness, gospel of peace, shield of faith, helmet of salvation, sword of the Spirit (Word).
