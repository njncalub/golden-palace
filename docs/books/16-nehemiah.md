# 16 - Nehemiah

**TLDR:** Nehemiah rebuilds Jerusalem's walls despite opposition, and with Ezra the people hear the law, repent, and renew the covenant. Leadership and community renewal in the land.

## Overarching Storyline

Nehemiah's burden and Artaxerxes' permission (ch. 1–2). Building the wall; opposition (ch. 3–6). List of returnees (ch. 7). Reading of the law; Feast of Booths (ch. 8). Confession and covenant (ch. 9–10). Population, dedication, reforms (ch. 11–13).

## Pegs for Memorizing This Book

- **Person:** Nehemiah, Ezra, Sanballat, Tobiah.
- **Image:** Wall, sword and trowel, open book of the law.
- **Number:** 52 (days to rebuild the wall), 12 (gates).
- **Phrase:** "The joy of the LORD is your strength" (8:10).

## Highlights

- Nehemiah 1:4–11 — Nehemiah's prayer.
- Nehemiah 4:17 — Building with one hand, weapon in the other.
- Nehemiah 8:10 — The joy of the LORD is your strength.
- Nehemiah 9 — Confession and covenant renewal.

## Before and After

- **Before:** Ezra restores temple and law; Nehemiah restores the walls and civic life.
- **After:** Esther is set in the Persian period (some overlap); Malachi closes the OT with prophecy.

## Place in the Overarching Biblical Story

**Wall and Word.** The city is secured; the people are anchored in the law and in covenant. The pattern of hearing, weeping, and rejoicing (ch. 8) is a model for revival.

## Interesting Facts

- **Cupbearer** — Nehemiah's role gave him access to the king; God uses his position.
- **Joy of the LORD** (8:10) — Often quoted for worship and encouragement.
